# Overview

The goal of every social intervention is improve some developmental outcomes. A **theory of change** is the causal path from inputs to outcomes.

For instance, let's assume the intervention is to give farmers access to better and timely advice which should improve farm yields. The theory of change from advice to yield improvement could be:

1. Context-aware advice will be made available for farmers

2. Farmers will access the advice

3. The advice will influence farmer behaviour

4. Farm yields will improve

:::tip What is the theory of change for your intervention?

Describe the the chain of events from inputs to outcomes. The chain can be longer than four steps!

:::

## Metrics

Instead of assuming that providing the right inputs will result in the right outcomes, its better to define metrics at each level that can be measured and tracked.

For instance, the farmer advice itervention could be measured at each step as such:

|   | Step                                                    | Metric                                                   |
| - | ------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 | Context-aware advice will be made available for farmers | <p>Advice accuracy</p><p>Relevance</p><p>Actionable</p>  |
| 2 | Farmers will access the advice                          | <p>Questions asked</p><p>Conversation length</p>         |
| 3 | The advice will influence farmer behaviour              | <p>Practices improved</p><p>Change in farming inputs</p> |
| 4 | Farm yields will improve                                | Crop cut                                                 |
