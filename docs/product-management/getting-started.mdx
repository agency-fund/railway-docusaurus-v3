# Getting Started

Good product management is a continuous loop: understand your users, measure how they behave, make changes, and validate those changes work. This section covers each part of that loop.

## The Loop

### 1. Understand your users → [User Research](./user-research)

Before building anything, know who you're building for and what problems they actually have. This means talking to users directly — through interviews, observation, and usability testing — not just reading analytics.

**Start here if:** you're launching a new product or feature, you're seeing unexpected drop-off, or you're not sure which problems to prioritise.

### 2. Measure what they do → [Product/Service Analytics](./product-service-analytics)

Once users are in your product, instrument it to track how they move through it — from first use (activation) to regular habit (retention). Analytics tells you *what* is happening at scale.

**Start here if:** you have a working product but no visibility into how users are actually using it.

### 3. Improve and test your changes → [Experimentation](./experimentation)

When you make changes, you need to know whether they worked. A/B testing and other experimentation methods let you confirm that improvements are real — not noise — before rolling them out to everyone.

**Start here if:** you're iterating on an existing product and want to be confident your changes are having the intended effect.

### 4. Build the team → [Hiring](./hiring)

As your product matures, you'll need the right people to own each part of this loop.

## Next Steps

If you're starting from scratch, work through these in order:

1. Run your first round of user research to define who you're building for
2. Instrument your product to track the user funnel
3. Build a metrics dashboard and assign ownership
4. Design your first experiment when you're ready to test a change
